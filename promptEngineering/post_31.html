How to Use Hotkeys in ChatGPT for Lightning-Fast Productivity

If you're tired of repeating the same prompts over and over again in ChatGPT, there's great news — you can now create your own hotkeys using Custom Instructions. This allows you to personalize your ChatGPT workflow and speed things up dramatically.

What Are Hotkeys in ChatGPT?

Hotkeys are short trigger phrases (like 5q or 3i) you set in the Custom Instructions section of ChatGPT. When you type the hotkey into a chat, ChatGPT instantly knows how to respond, without needing you to retype the full prompt each time.

Real-Life Example (Simplified)

Let's say you're working on blog articles. You often need inspiration for titles. Instead of typing "Suggest five catchy blog titles related to the current topic," every single time, just define a hotkey like this in Custom Instructions:

When I type '5t', please provide five creative blog title ideas based on the topic we’re discussing.

Now, anytime you're chatting with ChatGPT and type 5t, you'll immediately get title suggestions.

Why Is This Useful?

  • It saves you time on repeated prompts.
  • It keeps your sessions consistent.
  • You don’t need to copy/paste or remember long instructions.

More Hotkey Ideas to Try

  • 3a: Provide 3 alternative ways to approach this task.
  • 4p: Suggest 4 prompt improvements based on my question.
  • qz: Generate a quick quiz based on this topic.
  • 2ex: Give 2 real-world examples related to this subject.

How to Set Up Hotkeys

1. Click the three dots (...) in the bottom left corner of ChatGPT.
2. Select Custom Instructions.
3. In the box "How would you like ChatGPT to respond?", add your hotkey rules.

Limitations

You're currently limited to 1,500 characters in the Custom Instructions box. Prioritize your most useful hotkeys. Consider rotating them out as your needs change.

Pro Tip

If you work on multiple types of tasks (like writing, coding, teaching), organize your hotkeys with a prefix: w_ for writing, c_ for coding, etc. For example: w_5t for writing blog titles.

This small trick helps keep everything easy to manage.
