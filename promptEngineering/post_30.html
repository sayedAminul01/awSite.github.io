Boost ChatGPT Efficiency with Custom Instructions

If you've ever worked on a project that involved giving the same prompt to ChatGPT repeatedly, you know how frustrating it can be when the AI forgets your instructions mid-way. This is especially true for long conversations where context gets lost. Fortunately, there's a solution: Custom Instructions.

Real-World Example: Video Captioning Automation

Imagine you're working on a video series and need to identify moments in the script where text animations should appear. Originally, you'd paste your script, then explain what ChatGPT should do with it — highlight key phrases for animation, identify emotional beats, and so on. However, the longer the chat went on, the more the AI seemed to forget your original instructions.

With Custom Instructions, you can now set a default behavior for ChatGPT so it remembers what you're working on and how to respond, no matter how many new chats you start.

How to Set Up ChatGPT for Repetitive Tasks

  1. Open ChatGPT settings and click on Custom Instructions.
  2. In the box that asks what ChatGPT should know about you, explain your ongoing task. For example: "I regularly analyze video scripts to find 3–5 key moments for text animations based on keywords and emotional language."
  3. In the response behavior box, define what kind of output you want. For instance: "Respond with a bullet list of text animation moments, using the tone and pacing of the script."
  4. Save it. Now every new conversation will follow that pattern without repeating your prompt!

Example Custom Instruction

  • What should ChatGPT know?
    I am a video content editor analyzing scripts to identify powerful text animation points. I focus on emotional highs, key transitions, and impactful keywords.
  • How should ChatGPT respond?
    Provide 3–5 concise moments in a bulleted format, highlight the keyword or emotion, and briefly explain why that section stands out.

Why This Saves Time

  • Consistency: The AI remembers your format every time.
  • Focus: You only need to input your actual data — like the script or keywords — without repeating instructions.
  • Efficiency: You avoid the drop in quality that happens as the chat progresses and context fades.

Bonus Tips for Power Users

  • Update Often: Modify your Custom Instructions as your project evolves.
  • Split Use Cases: Use separate profiles or versions of your instructions for different types of tasks (e.g., one for writing, one for coding).
  • Stay Specific: Avoid vague phrases. Instead of "help me with editing," say "highlight passive voice and improve clarity."
